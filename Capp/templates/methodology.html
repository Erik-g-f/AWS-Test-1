Methodology

Here at MyEcoFriend we are committed to providing you with the most accurate representation of your carbon footprint possible. Therefore, we will in this section explain what numbers we use in our calculations as well as our formula. Our experience is that users trust the app more when they understand how it works. This knowledge will also allow the user to make their own calculations should they travel with a vehicle that they know to have a different emission level than the numbers we provide. Our app lets you calculate your carbon footprint from the cars, trains, motorbikes, commercial airplanes and ferries, as well as from walking and riding your bicycle which we assume have 0 emissions.

Calculations

The formula we use to calculate your carbon footprint derived from the average carbon footprint of each vehicle, the distance you travel, and the number of passengers in your vehicle. It can mathematically be expressed as follows:

\[ E = D \cdot V \div N \]

Where E = Emissions, D = Distance travelled, V = Vehicle's pollution/km, and N = Number of passengers in the vehicle (if applicable).

We have used the following numbers and formulas in our calculations:

Car

Medium Diesel Car:160g CO2/km

Medium Gasoline Car: 240g CO2/km

Electric SUV: 25g CO2/km

Small Electric Car: 14g CO2/km

Traffic significantly increases these numbers.

Total Carbon Emissions

To calculate the total carbon emissions from all the vehicles you have travelled with, we sum up the emissions from each vehicle using the following formula:

\[ TOTE = \sum_{i=1}^n E_i \]

Where TOTE = Total Carbon Emissions, and Ei = Emissions from each submission.

This number is further used to calculate how you are doing compared to the average in Norway and as we expand it will be used to calculate how you are doing compared to the average other countries as well.
